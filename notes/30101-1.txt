This is an 18 year old male who suffered a right MCA territory stroke secondary to traumatic dissection. He is left handed, and had aphasia on presentation, indicating that this patient is right brain dominant and had damage to his expressive language and partially his receptive language areas. His physical/occupational function and speech have improved. However, he still has significant sensorimotor impairment on the left and has limited function in his left hand, despite gains through outpatient rehab. His ability to function and adapt with use of his right hand has significantly improved since his last visit. Overall, his prognosis for functional ability is good with continued outpatient rehab. The progression of his stroke is being well managed with Eliquis now, and we will re-evaluate after further imaging.
